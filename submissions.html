Submit Your Voice

We’re looking for passionate, insightful, and creative student perspectives on science, medicine, mental health, ethics, engineering, and more. Whether it’s a personal narrative, an opinion piece, or a research-backed article, your voice matters here.

To submit: Please email your article as a PDF or Google Doc to youthfrontierjournal@gmail.com. Include your name, school, and grade or year in the email. Submissions are reviewed weekly, and if accepted, we’ll follow up with next steps!

Submission Guidelines

We welcome submissions on any scientific topic, from medicine and health to physics, engineering, ethics, and beyond. Our goal is to amplify youth voices in science by publishing thoughtful, well-researched, and original work.

Submission Categories
  • Reflections: Minimum 500 words. Includes personal narratives, opinion pieces grounded in evidence, and meaningful insights.
  • Features and Professional Papers: Minimum 1500 words. Includes research summaries, interviews with experts, and evidence-based analytical work.
Content Requirements
  • All work must be original and written by the student submitting.
  • Opinion pieces must be supported by credible evidence.
  • No creative nonfiction or fictional work is accepted.
  • Personal narratives must center around real scientific or healthcare-related experiences.
Formatting Checklist
  • Include a title and optional subtitle.
  • Include your full name and grade or year in school.
  • Use either APA or MLA style for citations.
  • Images, charts, or figures are encouraged (must be your own or properly credited).
Research Paper Structure
  • Title & Subtitle: Choose a clear title and optional subtitle to introduce your topic.
  • Introduction: Explain what your article is about, why it matters, and what question or topic you're addressing.
  • Main Body: Present your key points logically. Use subheadings if needed, and support your writing with examples, citations, or data.
  • Analysis or Discussion: Reflect on what your findings mean, add your insight, and acknowledge any limitations.
  • Conclusion: Summarize your main ideas and leave readers with something to think about or a direction for future research.
  • Citations: Use APA or MLA formatting. Be consistent and include a short reference list at the end.
